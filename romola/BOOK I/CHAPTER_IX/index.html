Summary: In this chapter of "Romola," Tito faces a moral dilemma after receiving a large sum of money from selling the gems. He debates whether he should use the money to search for his benefactor and possible father, Baldassarre Calvo, who might be a slave, or keep the money for himself and enjoy a comfortable life in Florence. Ultimately, Tito decides to assume that his father is dead, and he leaves the money with Cennini for safekeeping.

Time_period: 15th century (Renaissance)

As Cennini closed the door behind him, Tito turned round with the smiledying out of his face, and fixed his eyes on the table where theflorins lay.
